Bahrain's Top Attractions

Bahrain Fort

The Bahrain Fort, also called as Qal'at al Bahrain, is an ancient building built by the portuguese in Bahrain. The Fort has dated back to 2300 BC which was calculated after the uncovering of the fort site. The Bahrain Fort is preserved skilfully in order for tourists to discover and respect Bahrain history.

Image by Martin Falbisoner from Wikimedia Commons

Al Fateh Grand Mosque

The Al-Fateh Mosque is the largest mosque in Bahrain and is located in the city of Juffair. The mosque is made from several high quality materials including fibreglass, marble, teak wood. All these materials were imported to build the mosque.

Image by Martin Falbisoner from Wikimedia Commons

Bahrain International Circuit

The Bahrain International Circuit is a motorsport ground mainly used for Drag Racing and the yearly Bahrain Grand Prix. The International Circuit was opened in 2004 and since then has hosted the Bahrain Grand Prix annually.

Image by Finavon from Wikimedia Commons

Al Areen Wildlife Park & Reserve

The Al Areen Park is a reserve and a zoo located in Sakhir, Bahrain.It houses 7 km square of area and was first opened in 1976. It has more than 100,000 planted trees and almost 140 species of plants and animals together. The park's reserve can only be seen from a safe distance.

Image by Vort from Wikimedia Commons

Bahrain National Museum

The Bahrain National Museum is one of the oldest museums in Bahrain. The museum is one of the biggest in the kingdom and located in Manama. It was first established in 1988. The museum owns a vast collection of Bahrain's ancient artifacts collected since 1988. The artifacts date back to 5000 BC and are accessible to the public.
